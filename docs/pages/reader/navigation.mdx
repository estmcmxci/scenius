# Navigation & Progress

Paper Chat tracks your position in the paper and provides multiple navigation aids.

## Table of Contents

A fixed sidebar on the left lists all 8 paper sections:

1. Introduction
2. Related Work
3. Model & Methods
4. Results
5. Discussion
6. Limitations & Future Work
7. Conclusion
8. References

Click any section to smooth-scroll to it. The active section is highlighted with a left teal border and bold text.

### Animations

TOC items fade in with a staggered animation (40ms delay between items) when the component mounts, giving a cascading entrance effect.

## Section Tracking

The `useIntersectionTracker` hook uses the Intersection Observer API to detect which section is currently in view:

- **Root margin:** `-20% 0px -70% 0px` — a section is considered "active" when its top edge enters the upper 20% of the viewport
- **Threshold:** `0` — triggers as soon as any part of the section crosses the margin

All `[data-section]` elements are observed. When a new section enters the detection zone, the context updates and three things happen simultaneously:

1. The **TOC** highlights the new section
2. The **header** updates the section label with a fade animation
3. The **suggested prompts** below the chat change to match the section

## Reading Progress Bar

A 2px teal bar at the top of the paper panel shows how far you've scrolled. It's computed as a fraction of total scroll height:

```
progress = scrollTop / (scrollHeight - clientHeight)
```

The bar width scales from 0% to 100% as you scroll through the paper.

## Scroll-to-Section

When you click a TOC entry, the app calls `scrollToSection(slug)` from the paper reader context. This:

1. Finds the `[data-section="slug"]` element
2. Scrolls the paper panel container to bring it into view with `scrollIntoView({ behavior: 'smooth' })`
3. The intersection observer then picks up the change and updates the active state
